LANGUAGE PROJECTS




1. Spanish Sounds (My observations)


    Vowels: There are 5 sounds: a, e, i, o, u

  • a: similar to 'a' in English 'father' but move the sound further to the front
  • e: as in English bed
  • i: as in English eat
  • o: as in English oar
  • u: as in English cool

    Consonants:

  • r, the tongue roller, doesn't exist in English so it might be difficult for some. If unable to do it, pronounce it as l (el). It sounds closer with l than the English r.
  • v is pronounced as b or soft b (by lightly touching the lips). Hard b in the beginning of the sentence and can be either hard or soft b elsewhere.
  • l (el) sound is similar to English l but you need to make sure the tongue tip touches the roof of the mouth and keep it there a little bit longer.
  • d is not pronounced as English d. Rather it’s like the “th” sound as in “the.” You can also pronounce it by placing the tongue tip just behind the upper teeth.
  • t is pronounced as t in English word “style.” But I also hear a very small percentage of people (maybe 5 percent?) pronounce it as t in English “tile.” Same with k (as in sky) and p (as in spy)
  • g is similar to English g but make it a little noisier, more like g in girl than in guess.
