2. Questions


   Big Questions

  • Does heaven exist?
    • Quran: “gardens of pleasure,” there are rivers of wine. (Yes)
    • Anglican Bishop Stephen Lowe: I don't know. (Maybe)
    • Stephen Hawking: There is no heaven; it's a fairy story. (No)
    • Ancient Greeks: Yes, it's called Elysium. Achilles was in heaven but he told Odysseus who was visiting him it's boring there. (Yes, but not what you think)
      (Achilles' words from "The Odyssey": By god, I’d rather slave on earth for another man . . .than rule down here over all the breathless dead.)
    • Bart Ehrman, a bible scholar, on "Fresh Air" with Terry Gross: People today, when they read the phrase kingdom of God, they think he (Jesus) is talking about heaven, the place that your soul goes to when you die. But Jesus isn't talking about heaven because he doesn't believe - he's a Jew - he doesn't believe in the separation of soul and body. (Really?)

   Small Questions

  • Is there beer in heaven?
    • Beer Song: "hey, hey, hey, in heaven, there is no beer. That is why we be drinking all the beer, la, la, la..." (No)
    • Clement: You can't call it heaven if there is no beer. (Therefore: yes, there is.)

5. Interesting TED Talks/Clips/Articles


  • The Paradox of Choice   -Barry Schwartz    (TED Talk)
    • Psychologist Barry Schwartz takes aim at a central tenet of western societies: freedom of choice. In Schwartz's estimation, choice has made us not freer but more paralyzed, not happier but more dissatisfied.

  • The transformative power of classical music   -Benjamin Zander    (TED Talk)
    • Benjamin Zander has two infectious passions: classical music, and helping us all realize our untapped love for it -- and by extension, our untapped love for all new possibilities, new experiences, new connections.

  • Why you think you're right - even if you're wrong   -Julia Galef    (TED Talk)
    • Perspective is everything, especially when it comes to examining your beliefs. Are you a soldier, prone to defending your viewpoint at all costs -- or a scout, spurred by curiosity?
    So, be a scout if you care about the truth. Be a soldier if you want to be popular.

  • Why we shouldn't trust markets with our civic life   -Michael Sandel    (TED Talk)
    • In the past three decades, says Michael Sandel, the US has drifted from a market economy to a market society; it's fair to say that an American's experience of shared civic life depends on how much money they have. (Three key examples: access to education, access to justice, political influence.) In a talk and audience discussion, Sandel asks us to think honestly on this question: In our current democracy, is too much for sale?

  • Looks aren't Everything, Believe me I'm a model   -Cameron Russell    (TED Talk)
    • Cameron Russell admits she won "a genetic lottery": she's tall, pretty and an underwear model. But don't judge her by her looks. In this fearless talk, she takes a wry look at the industry that had her looking highly seductive at barely 16 years old.

  • The moral roots of liberals and conservatives   -Jonathan Haidt    (TED Talk)
    • Psychologist Jonathan Haidt studies the five moral values that form the basis of our political choices, whether we're left, right or center. In this eye-opening talk, he pinpoints the moral values that liberals and conservatives tend to honor most.

  • Does Heaven Exist?   -The Big Questions    (YouTube 1)   (YouTube 2)
    • Serious but lighthearted discussions about heaven. Interesting answers at 0:57 of clip1 and 4:45 of clip2. Nicky Campbell is a witty, fair host.
